| Stakeholder | Role | Interest Level | Influence Level |

## 2. Stakeholder Profiles

### 2.1 Product Manager

**Responsibilities:**
- Product vision and strategy alignment
- Prioritization and scope management
- Stakeholder communication
- Success metrics definition

**Expectations:**
- Regular progress updates
- Risk escalation
- Data-driven decision making

**Communication Preferences:**
- Weekly status reports
- Bi-weekly stakeholder meetings
- Ad-hoc for blockers

### 2.2 Engineering Lead

**Responsibilities:**
- Technical architecture and design
- Development planning and estimation
- Code quality and standards
- Technical risk assessment

**Expectations:**
- Clear technical specifications
- Resource requirements
- Timeline accuracy

**Communication Preferences:**
- Daily stand-ups
- Technical design reviews
- Sprint planning

### 2.3 Pilot Creators (Key External Stakeholders)

**Profile:**
- 10 selected MacroActive creators
- Have influence on broader creator community
- Provide feedback on feature usefulness

**Expectations:**
- Early access to features
- Support during pilot
- Revenue impact from improved retention

**Communication Preferences:**
- Weekly pilot updates
- Dedicated support channel
- Monthly feedback sessions

### 2.4 End Users

**Profile:**
- Fitness app subscribers
- Varying engagement levels
- Motivated by progress and achievement

**Expectations:**
- Fair and accurate streak tracking
- Visible recognition (badges)
- Minimal disruption to existing experience

## 3. RACI Matrix

| Activity | Product Manager | Engineering Lead | QA Lead | Designer | Data Analyst | CS Team |
|----------|----------------|------------------|---------|----------|--------------|---------|
| Requirements Definition | A/R | C | I | C | I | I |
| Technical Design | C | A/R | C | I | I | I |
| UI/UX Design | C | C | I | A/R | I | I |
| Development | I | A/R | C | I | I | I |
| Testing | I | C | A/R | I | I | C |
| Deployment Approval | A | C | A | I | I | I |
| Creator Onboarding | A/R | C | I | I | I | A/R |
| Data Analysis | A | C | I | I | A/R | I |

**Legend:**
- **A** = Accountable (single point of accountability)
- **R** = Responsible (performs the work)
- **C** = Consulted (provides input)
- **I** = Informed (kept up to date)

## 5. Stakeholder Engagement Strategy

### 5.1 High Influence / High Interest (Manage Closely)

**Stakeholders:** Product Manager, Engineering Lead, Pilot Creators

**Strategy:**
- Weekly formal communication
- Direct access to project team
- Involvement in key decisions
- Early access to features

### 5.2 High Influence / Medium Interest (Keep Satisfied)

**Stakeholders:** Platform Team, QA Lead, Customer Success

**Strategy:**
- Regular updates (bi-weekly)
- Consulted on key decisions
- Clear escalation path

### 5.3 Medium Influence / High Interest (Keep Informed)

**Stakeholders:** Designers, Data Analyst

**Strategy:**
- Included in relevant discussions
- Access to project artifacts
- Regular status updates

### 5.4 Medium Influence / Medium Interest (Monitor)

**Stakeholders:** Broader Creator Community

**Strategy:**
- General updates through normal channels
- No direct project involvement
- Feedback gathered through support channels

## 6. Feedback Collection

### 6.1 Pilot Creator Feedback

**Methods:**
- Weekly check-in calls
- In-app feedback mechanism
- Monthly structured survey

**Key Questions:**
- How has this impacted your engagement metrics?
- What features are most/least valuable?
- What would make this more useful?
- Any bugs or issues encountered?

### 6.2 End User Feedback

**Methods:**
- In-app NPS surveys
- Feedback form on badge earning
- App store review analysis

**Key Questions:**
- Does streak tracking motivate you?
- How do you feel about badge visibility?
- Any frustration with streak mechanics?

## 7. Issue Escalation

### 7.1 Escalation Matrix

| Severity | Response Time | Escalation Path |
|----------|---------------|-----------------|
| Critical (P0) | 1 hour | Engineering Lead → Product Manager → Sponsor |
| High (P1) | 4 hours | Engineering Lead → Product Manager |
| Medium (P2) | 1 business day | Team Lead |
| Low (P3) | Next sprint | Product Backlog |

## 8. Change Management

### 8.1 Stakeholder Change Approval

| Change Type | Approval Required |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Scope Change | Product Manager, Sponsor |
| Timeline Change | Engineering Lead, Product Manager |
| Resource Change | Engineering Manager, Product Manager |
| Priority Change | Product Manager |

### 8.2 Communication of Changes

1. Document change in project artifacts
2. Email notification to affected stakeholders
3. Discussion in next scheduled meeting
4. Update action items and dependencies
